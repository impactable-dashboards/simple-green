Section 08
Findings and Recommendations
10 findings from the audience data, ranked by strategic importance.
ICP Targeting Score: 78/100
Averaged across all 4 dimensions (industry, size, function, seniority), 78.4% of clicks go to ICP-aligned segments. Company size is the strongest dimension at 98.2%. Industry is the weakest at 64.6%.
QA and Ops Dominate the Buying Committee
Quality Assurance (33%) and Operations (31%) account for 64% of all clicks. These are the right functions: QA evaluates compliance and safety, Operations controls budgets and vendor selection. This validates the targeting thesis.
Mid-Market Sweet Spot Confirmed
Companies with 201-5,000 employees account for 79.4% of all clicks. The 1,001-5,000 band alone drives 34%. This matches Simple Green's ICP perfectly: large enough to have procurement processes, small enough that Diversey/Ecolab are overkill.
Entry-Level Seniority Consumes 27% of Clicks
Entry-level personas (QA Technicians, Safety Specialists, QC Inspectors) drive 366 clicks at 26.8% share. They are the lowest CPC ($18.68) but are unlikely purchase decision-makers. Evaluate whether their influence value justifies the spend, or if seniority-based bid adjustments should redirect budget to Manager and Director levels.
VP and CXO Engagement Is Minimal
VP accounts for only 2.6% of clicks (36), CXO just 0.8% (11). These are the economic buyers who sign contracts. The system reaches them (12,914 impressions combined) but they rarely click. Consider Thought Leader ad format to reach executives in-feed with founder content.
Non-ICP Industries Absorb 35% of Clicks
Hospitals/Healthcare (110 clicks), Business Consulting (67 clicks), and Construction (59 clicks) are outside core ICP but receiving significant engagement. Healthcare may be adjacent (clean room environments), but Business Consulting and Construction are likely waste. Evaluate for exclusion.
Purchasing Function Is Underrepresented
Purchasing/Procurement accounts for only 6.6% of clicks despite being a primary economic buyer. This function has fewer people on LinkedIn in manufacturing, so lower volume is expected, but dedicated creative addressing procurement pain points (SKU consolidation, vendor simplification, LEED compliance) could improve engagement.
Sales Function Leakage Unresolved
Sales function shows 42 clicks (2.4%) and $793 in spend. Previously flagged in the Company Recon as generating conversion events despite not being targeted. Decision needed: exclude (cleaner data) or investigate (distribution/channel partners may hold Sales titles in industrial).
Food and Beverage Manufacturing Is the Top Performer
F&B Manufacturing leads all industries with 374 clicks (11.7%), 0.50% CTR, and $20.39 CPC. Combined with F&B Services and Wholesale F&B, the food vertical accounts for 679 clicks (21.2%). This is the strongest signal for audience concentration.
Operations Is the Only Function Converting
Of all job functions, only Operations has generated conversions (6 at $1,801 each). Quality Assurance, despite driving 33% of clicks, has zero conversions. This could indicate: (a) Operations personas are further down the buying journey, (b) the conversion event measures something Operations-specific (website page visit patterns), or (c) QA clicks are research behavior that does not yet convert. Needs investigation.
